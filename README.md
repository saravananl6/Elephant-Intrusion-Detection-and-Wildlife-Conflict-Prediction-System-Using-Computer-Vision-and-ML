Overview

The Elephant Intrusion Detection and Wildlife Conflict Prediction System is an intelligent wildlife monitoring and conservation platform designed to reduce human-elephant conflicts by detecting elephant movements and predicting potential intrusion risks in vulnerable areas.

The system combines Computer Vision, Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, Weather Analytics, Geospatial Intelligence, and Predictive Forecasting to provide early warnings about elephant activity near agricultural lands, villages, and protected forest boundaries.

Using surveillance cameras, uploaded videos, environmental conditions, historical intrusion records, moon phase analysis, seasonal patterns, and weather information, the platform automatically detects elephant presence and forecasts future intrusion probabilities.

When an elephant is detected, the system immediately generates alerts, stores evidence snapshots, records intrusion details, and sends notification emails to registered users.

Unlike traditional monitoring systems that only detect wildlife after an event occurs, this platform also predicts future elephant movement risks, enabling proactive prevention and wildlife management.
